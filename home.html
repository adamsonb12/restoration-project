For the Saints:
The Lord's Commandments, in the Latter Days
By Brett Adamson

The Ten Commandments

After being called as a holy prophet and leading his people out of bondage towards safety, in the the book of Exodus, chapter 20, Moses the received the 10 commandments directly from God. God gave the commandments to Moses in order to help Moses guide, govern, and lead his people, who were the house of Israel.

The Ten Commandments given to Moses are as follows:

  • Thou shalt have no other gods before Me
  • Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image
  • Thou shalt not take the name of the Lord thy God in vain
  • Remember the Sabbath day to keep it holy
  • Honour thy father and mother
  • Thou shalt not kill
  • Thou shalt not commit adultery
  • Thou shalt not steal
  • Thou shalt not bear false witness
  • Thou shalt not covet

These commandments still exist today, and are revered as eternal law in most Christian religions; however, if Moses had existed in our day, charged with teaching and leading modern people today, would those commandments be any different? Moses lived in an ancient time, leading people who had been slaves all their lives to newfound freedom. The people were leaving the great civilization of Egypt, to start fresh and form their own new nation. Their lives, and their circumstances have very little in common with ours today. For christian saints in these latter days, how would God’s revelation of his commandments change, based on his people’s circumstances and time? Of course the ten commandments are a foundation, but God has already built upon his ten commandments, revealing His law for His church to Joseph Smith the prophet.

Overview of Joseph Smith

Joseph Smith is the founder of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. At age 14, he started having revelations from God. As time went on, Joseph received more revelations and was called by God to be His prophet, and to restore His true church. As Joseph continued to build up God’s restored kingdom, he received many more revelations about doctrine, teachings, and various other commandments. Joseph recorded many of these revelations in the Doctrine and Covenants, now regarded as a book of scripture by the LDS church.

In section 42 of the Doctrine and Covenants, Joseph recorded a revelation he received, where God listed and explained the commandments He wanted His church and His saints to obey. These commandments build upon the ten commandments given in ancient time, and are God’s law today. Members of the LDS church should study these and see them as equals to the ten commandments. All christians should study these commandments and follow them, to draw closer to Christ.
